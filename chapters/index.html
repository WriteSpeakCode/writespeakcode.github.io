Organizers

    Jeca Armstrong is a Golang developer at a software consulting company and co-founder of the NYC Write/Speak/Code Meetup. She's passionate about multicultural perspectives, helping marginalized groups empower themselves, and intersectionalism. A multidisciplinary enthusiast, her background is in labor and environmental politics, gardening, and antiques. Her love of travel has led her to visit 17 countries on five continents (so far).

    Jessica Simon is a software engineer at a fin tech startup called Gust. She is passionate about innovation, design, education, and all things tech! During her free time, you can find her flipping around the gymnastics gym, cheering on the Maryland Terrapins, discovering new restaurants, and traveling around the world.

    Lisa van Gelder has over 15 years experience in software engineering, including leading and managing software development teams. Her career has taken her between London and New York at companies ranging from small startups to large media organizations, including the Guardian and the BBC. She is currently VP of Engineering at Stride. Lisa lives in Brooklyn and is mostly powered by coffee.

Organizers

    Caroline Artz is a full-stack web developer and designer in Chicago. She enjoys mentoring students at Dev Bootcamp and has instructed the Ruby on Rails back-end development track at Startup Institute, Chicago. Beyond coding, she enjoys traveling, strong coffee, graphic design, and photo editing.

    Dalal Al Rayes is a full stack developer with a background in finance and private equity. She has helped companies increase their value by turning around their operations, restructuring management and redefining products. Dalal holds an MBA from London Business School and is a recent Dev Bootcamp graduate. She wants to live in a world where competition is fair, high heels are comfortable, and open source is more accessible to beginners. When she's not coding, Dalal is biking around Chicago trying to find the best peanut butter cookies.

    Rebecca Miller-Webster is a software engineer, conference organizer, and teacher. She is the founder of Write/Speak/Code and Managing Director for thoughtbot Chicago. Rebecca has been developing software professionally for over 10 years and previously organized GORUCO. She was the founding teacher at Dev Bootcamp NYC and has taught hundreds of students software development as well as led workshops on public speaking, leadership, and oppression. Rebecca holds an Masters in Computer Science and a BA in Women and Gender Studies from Washington University in St. Louis and was named one of 7 Brillant Women in Tech by Craig Nemark, founder of Craigslist. She loves cupcakes, sea mammals, and prosecco. Rebecca lives in Oak Park, IL with her husband, black pug, and rescued havenese. And she changes her hair. A lot.
